Boston energy firm to partner with new Tesla battery system

By Jon Chesto Globe Staff  May 01, 2015

With its stock languishing for the past year, Boston’s EnerNOC Inc. could really use a powerful ally in its mission to rethink how companies use electricity.

Fortunately for EnerNOC, that friend unveiled himself on Thursday: He’s Tesla Motors chief executive Elon Musk, the bold billionaire who is trying to put batteries in our cars and people in outer space.

Continue reading below

As Tesla rolls out its new “Powerpack” battery storage systems for industrial and commercial users, EnerNOC will provide energy intelligence software to make those batteries work efficiently. This is how EnerNOC chief executive Tim Healy puts it: Tesla will provide the muscle for these customers’ energy systems, and EnerNOC will provide the brains.

EnerNOC investors seemed encouraged by the news, driving up the company’s stock price by more than 20 percent by noon on Friday, to nearly $13.50 a share.

The Tesla-EnerNOC partnership will begin slowly, through pilot programs in California. One of the first customers is Stater Bros. Markets, a southern California supermarket chain.

Tesla plugs into new market

Never lacking daring ideas, billionaire entrepreneur Elon Musk is determined to jolt the electricity market.

EnerNOC’s software will help users know when to stop drawing power from the grid and instead draw from the Tesla battery packs, based on a customer’s usage patterns and the fluctuations in wholesale electricity prices that are driven in large part by times of peak demand. The software could also be used to help companies with solar panels to more effectively deploy the power from those panels.

Healy said EnerNOC’s discussions with Tesla began last year. He expects the relationship could bring tens of millions of dollars in revenue to EnerNOC over time.

“I think there’s a key thread of commonality between Elon Musk and a number of us here at EnerNOC,” Healy said. “They’re in the business to try the change how the world uses energy. ... In order to make big changes occur, you have to have big bold ideas, and we like that.”
