One amazing day of sports on tap Saturday

The Boston Globe Share Tweet

By Andrew Mahoney Globe Staff  May 01, 2015

After a long, miserable winter, spring has finally arrived in Boston. With temperatures expected to be in the 60s, Saturday looks like a perfect day to get some yard work done. Maybe there are some other outdoor projects that need tending.

Or perhaps you can take advantage of the nice weather to play catch with the kids, or take the dogs for a nice leisurely stroll.

Continue reading below

Or not.

Those are all admirable goals, but there are just too many can’t miss sporting events on television Saturday. My family is going to have to accept that I am going to be more useless than usual this weekend. For those who do wish to partake in any of the previously mentioned activities, you’ll want to be done by noon.

The NFL, NHL, NBA, and MLB will all be in action, while both horse racing and boxing look to take center stage. Heck, even the Revolution get in on it with a game against the Red Bulls, which the boss tells me is an important game.

Fight night, but not in bars

Many bars won’t show Saturday’s bout between Floyd Mayweather and Manny Pacquiao because it’s too expensive.

If you find all of this overwhelming, don’t worry, here’s a breakdown of what’s on, and when to watch it.

Noon: NFL Draft, Rounds 4-7 on NFL Network and ESPN

Jim Davis/Globe Staff File

The Patriots selected David Givens in the seventh round out of Notre Dame in 2002. He went on to become a starting wide receiver for the Patriots’ Super Bowl teams in 2003 and 2004.

Sure, the first three rounds will already be in the books, but there are plenty of bargains to be had in the later rounds. Tom Brady of course was drafted in the sixth round in 2000 with the 199th pick. He’s not the only Super Bowl MVP selected that late, as the Denver Broncos struck gold by taking Terrell Davis with the 196th pick in 1995. Davis went on to become the Broncos’ all-time leading rusher in an injury-shortened career while leading Denver to back-to-back Super Bowl titles in 1997 and 1998.

Some of Brady’s favorite targets have also come late in the draft. Wide receivers Julian Edelman and David Givens were taken in the seventh round, while Troy Brown was selected in the eighth. (The NFL does not even have an eighth round anymore).

12:30 p.m.: Washington Capitals at New York Rangers, NBC

Getty Images

The New York Rangers and the Washington Capitals are just one game into their series, but both teams already appear to be under each other’s skin.

It’s only Game 2 of this Eastern Conference semifinal, but it feels like a must win for the Rangers, who do not want to go down 2-0 at home. The Capitals won a thriller in Game 1 Thursday night. The game looked to he heading to overtime when Joel Ward scored with 1.3 seconds left off a nice feed from Alex Ovechkin to give Washington the 2-1 win. It was a perfect start to the second round of the playoffs.

1:35 p.m.: Yankees at Red Sox, NESN

Barry Chin/Globe Staff File

Alex Rodriguez and the New York Yankees are in town for a three-game series.

The Yankees make their first trip to Fenway this year. The Red Sox enter the series just one game behind the Yankees for first place in what has been avery mediocre AL East so far. Alex Rodriguez comes to Boston just one home run shy of tying Willie Mays for fourth on the all-time list. Dan Shaughnessy calls it Awkwardness Personified.

6:34 p.m.: 141st Kentucky Derby, NBC

Getty Images

American Pharoah has rattled off four impressive victories with big speed figures, then had an eye-popping workout Sunday.

The first Saturday in May is always exciting for horse racing fans, but there is an increased buzz surrounding this year’s race. Joe Sullivan explained why in Thursday’s Globe.

“A bettor could make a case for 10 horses in this year’s Derby, which makes this a difficult gambling proposition. The favorites are all legitimate, too. The pace — usually hotly contested in the Derby — will be critical, and if it’s too fast, the late runners could take advantage. The favorites seem so talented that the winner probably will come from that group, and it will be the one who has the best trip, not too fast and avoiding traffic.”

The actual race is at 6:34 p.m., but NBC’s coverage starts at 4 p.m., so if you’re with a group that likes to sip mint juleps and watch celebrities walking the red carpet and wearing silly hats (and who doesn’t, really?), tune in early. The broadcast will also feature a taped segment from Boston sportscaster Bob Neumeier, back at work after recovering from a stroke.

Looking to wager? Joe Sullivan has handicapped the 2015 Kentucky Derby and makes his picks.

7:30 p.m.: Red Bulls at Revolution, CSN

AP

The Revolution clinched the Eastern Conference last year at Gillette Stadium against the Red Bulls.

This is a rematch of last year’s Eastern Conference Championship, won by the Revolution. And also, it’s part two of a Boston-New York rivalry doubleheader after the Red Sox-Yankees game.

Once again, they are two of the top teams in Eastern Conference of the MLS, as the Revs sport a record of 4-2-2, while the Red Bulls are 3-0-4.

8p.m.: San Antonio Spurs vs. Los Angeles Clippers, Game 7, ESPN

AP

Blake Griffin and Tim Duncan will square off Saturday night to see who moves on to the Western conference semifinals.

This looked to be the most intriguing matchup of the first round of the NBA playoffs. The Spurs are the defending champions, squaring off against a Clippers squad that is trying to prove it is one of the best teams in the league.

The teams have certainly delivered, as it is the only series to go to a Game 7. And this has not been your typical NBA playoff series with the home team holding serve. Both the Spurs and the Clippers have won twice on the road.

9 p.m.: Floyd Mayweather Jr. vs. Manny Pacquiao, PPV

REUTERS

Why are these two smiling? Because Saturday night’s fight is expected to gross at least $400 million.

True, this fight should have happened at least five years ago, and yes, the $100 price tag to watch on television is steep. Still, it’s the fight everyone has been clamoring for. Those who haven’t made plans to view the fight by now are probably in full panic mode as they try to either invite themselves over a friend’s house to watch, or convince their housemates why ponying up to buy the fight is a good decision. One option that won’t be available is to watch it in a bar, as most establishments consider the proposition to be too expensive. The undercard starts at 9 p.m., but don’t be surprised if the main event doesn’t get going until after midnight.
